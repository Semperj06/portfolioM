Phenomenon of Goal Deficit or Target Vacuum

Target Vacuum, or the "Phenomenon of Goal Deficit," is a significant phenomenon observed across various contexts and societal spheres. In our work, we focus on this phenomenon as it directly relates to companies and their employees, which is the primary focus of our research.

The Target Vacuum is characterized by the absence of clear and achievable goals, both for companies and their employees. For companies, the lack of well-defined goals can lead to a range of negative consequences, including decreased employee motivation, deteriorating management and planning, challenges in performance evaluation, increased turnover rates, and missed growth opportunities.

Conversely, the problem of target vacuum among employees can result in a loss of motivation, engagement in trivial tasks during work hours, the creation of imaginary goals, and the rationalization of their fears, which may differ from the company's real objectives. As a result, company resources may be directed towards achieving unrealistic goals, ultimately becoming a source of losses and hindering the achievement of the company's true goals and objectives.

The importance of aligning personal and corporate goals is highlighted as a crucial aspect in dealing with the target vacuum. Valuable employees are those who can align their personal ambitions with the goals and values of the company, ensuring a harmonious interaction between individual and corporate growth.

Introduction to Strategic Planning and Causes of Target Vacuum

I trust it is no longer a matter of doubt for you that the Target Vacuum is an undeniable issue. I would go as far as to say that the Target Vacuum is The Silent Barrier To Organizational Success. It's crucial to grasp that managing a company, managing a project—these involve managing strategic goals, the dynamics of goals, and, of course, conveying these goals to its employees.

The deficit of goals can stem from various reasons, including:

  1. Insufficient strategic efforts: Companies fail to conduct regular strategic assessments and update their goals in line with evolving market conditions.
  2. Ambiguous goal formulations: Company goals lack specificity, measurability, achievability, relevance, and time-bound attributes.
  3. Lack of goal support from leadership: Management fails to demonstrate commitment to the goals and does not create conducive conditions for their achievement.
  4. Ineffective communication channels: Companies struggle to effectively communicate goals to employees due to ineffective communication channels or insufficient communication.
  5. Lack of feedback: Obtaining feedback from employees about the set goals, their understanding, and ways to achieve them is essential.

Strategic Management - Goals Definition

Strategic management involves strategic sessions where the company's mission and objectives are crafted and refined. These sessions serve as a platform for verbalizing corporate values, culture, and goals, incorporating SWOT analysis and including:

  • • Transformation of strengths, weaknesses, and threats into opportunities.
  • • Assessment of opportunities.
  • • Brainstorming.
  • • Dialectical approach (TRIZ).
  • • Identifying key success factors and the business mission.
